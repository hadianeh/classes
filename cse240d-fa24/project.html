Objective

You are required to complete a course project. The project can be related to any of the topics discussed in the class. A strong project will demonstrate understanding of topics beyond the scope of what we covered in class. The objective is to develop a work that can be eventually extended to a conference paper. Innovation is valued above all; your execution of the idea is next most important. As researchers, you should be to develop an idea to a submission and meet the conference deadlines.

Requirements

To enable students to develop complete and working projects in a timely manner, the only hard requirement for project ideas are they must be built on top of the GeneSys framework. The reason being is, because my lab developed the project from the ground up over the last few years, I and my students have a deep understanding of the framework and can provide support and guidance to the students in a way not possible with other frameworks.

Project Partners

You are required to work in groups of two. If you are looking for a partner, send a message in the "partner-finder" channel on Slack.

Project Proposal

You are required to submit a project proposal to Gradescope by 11:59 PM on 10/18/2024. The project proposal can be in any format that you like as long as it is submitted as a PDF. I suggest including a timeline for the project in the proposal along with figures illustrating the proposed solution. Additionally, the project must include the following information:

  • What research problem the project is solving.
  • Why this specific research problem is important.
  • How the team will solve the research problem.

    Project Presentation

    You are required to present your project to the class. The same guidelines that apply for the paper presentations apply for the project presentation (located on the presentation page). To restate the important guidelines, the presentation should be 15 minutes long. Each presentation will be followed by a 5-10 minute Q&A session. You should present your project as if you were presenting at a conference. However, unlike the paper presentations, you should discuss your empirical results in detail along with technical details concerning your implementation. Be prepared to show code during your presentation.

    Grading

    I will evaluate your talk on a a scale of one to ten.

    Clear Presentation of Key Ideas 4 points
    Clear, Well-Organized Slides 4 points
    Drawing High-Level Insights from Empirical Results 1 point
    Motivating Future Work 1 point

    Project Report

    The project report should be a PDF with no more than six pages of primary content using the ISCA LaTeX template. You are allowed unlimited space for the citations and appendices, starting on page seven, but your report should be complete and understandable without reading this extra material.

    Grading

    I will evaluate your report on a a scale of one to ten. If your report is ready for submission to a conference or workshop (not in format, but in content; it should already be in the correct format), you will receive a 10% bonus to your final grade; this will be determined subjectively by me (Hadi).

    Organized Communication of Ideas 4 points
    Empirical Support for Proposed Technique 3 points
    Grammar and Spelling 1 point
    Clarity and Grace (i.e., clear, well organized arguments in well organized paragraphs) 2 points

    Project Implementation

    You should demonstrate that your implementation works using both micro benchmarks and larger, potentially end-to-end, benchmarks. There are no hard requirements for the implementation other than it must be built on top of the GeneSys. Additionally, there are no hard requirements for the benchmarks you should use; reference the papers we have read in class for ideas or ask the TA (Christopher Priebe) if you are really stuck.

    Grading

    You will get a score between zero to ten based on the completeness of your implementation.
