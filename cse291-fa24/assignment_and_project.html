3. Job Talk Assignment (Due: todo)

PhD Students/MS Students with Research Experience

  • Job Talk Preparation: Prepare your own job talk presentation (15-20 minutes).
  • Presentation Slides: Submit your presentation slides to Canvas before the deadline.
  • In-Class Presentation: Present your job talk during a designated class session.

Master's (MS) Students

  • Job Talk Critique: Watch Amy Zhang's job talk (link provided) or another online job talk.
  • Job Talk Analysis: Analyze the presentation based on content, structure, delivery, and engagement. Identify strengths and areas for improvement.
  • Critique Report: Write a 500-750 word report addressing the following:
    • Introduction: Evaluation of speaker introduction and topic.
    • Content: Clarity and relevance of the content.
    • Structure: Organization and use of visual aids.
    • Delivery: Assessment of speaker's communication skills.
    • Overall Impression: Effectiveness of the job talk and presentation of expertise.
    • Recommendations: Constructive suggestions for improvement.
  • Submission: Upload your report to Canvas.

Final Project (Due: todo)


Project Description

The final project is a research-based project that demonstrates your understanding of the course material and your ability to apply it to a real-world academic setting. You will work independently on this project.

Project Topics

Choose one of the following project topics:

  • Research Statement Analysis: Analyze multiple research statements from different fields and identify common themes, strengths, and weaknesses.
  • Teaching Philosophy Development: Develop a comprehensive teaching philosophy statement based on your experiences and understanding of effective teaching practices.
  • Academic Job Market Research: Conduct research on academic job markets in your desired field, including trends, expectations, and application strategies.
  • Faculty Mentoring: Explore the role of faculty mentors in supporting graduate students and junior faculty members.
  • Academic Writing and Publishing: Analyze the process of academic writing and publishing, including guidelines, peer review, and ethical considerations.

Project Deliverables

  • Project Proposal: Submit a brief project proposal outlining your chosen topic, research questions, and methodology.
  • Final Report: Write a comprehensive report summarizing your findings, analysis, and conclusions.
  • Presentation: Present your project findings to the class in a 15-minute presentation.

Grading

The final project will be graded based on the quality of your research, analysis, writing, and presentation.
