Course Description

The course offers a thorough exploration of the academic job market, specifically designed for those aspiring to secure assistant professor positions at universities. Whether you are targeting a research-oriented role or a teaching-focused position, this course will provide you with the essential tools, strategies, and insights needed to succeed. You will learn how to craft compelling application materials, including your CV, cover letters, research statements, and teaching philosophies. Throughout the course, you'll receive personalized feedback, engage in mock interviews, and refine your presentation skills.

In addition to mastering the application process, the course gives you a realistic understanding of what it means to be a professor. You will explore topics such as work-life balance, tenure expectations, and navigating departmental dynamics. By the end of the course, you will be well-prepared to present yourself as a strong candidate and confidently embark on your academic career.

Course Format

The course will consist of lectures and student activities (see Participation). Some lectures will be from new faculty members, professors, and department chairs. You will have the opportunity to ask questions and gain valuable insights. Additionally, there will be dedicated sessions where you can work on your own application materials, receiving feedback and guidance from instructors and peers.
